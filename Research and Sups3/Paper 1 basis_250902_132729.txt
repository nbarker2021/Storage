Abstract
We present a compact instruction set architecture (ISA) that organizes data transformations across eight layers (n=1…n=8) using a single algebraic core: digits in , quad-level legality constraints (ALT, W4, Q8), a helicity phase tag , isometric symmetries (Mirror/Dual/Inverse), and a 16 + 1 carry law that isolates phase changes to boundary receipts. Two unifying optimization principles—CLTMP (Combined Lowest Taxicab Meeting Point) and E₈ quantization—guide minimal work plans and canonical resting states. We formalize and test an Anti-Chiral Obstruction Criterion (ACOC) that detects intrinsic chirality defects (orientation-independent). External mathematics (optimal E₈ sphere packing and kissing number; knot-theoretic subadditivity of unknotting number; Ten/Dry Martini spectral dualities) aligns with the architecture’s predictions about symmetry neighborhoods, bundled edits, and CRT/duality openings of “gates.” We provide harnesses, datasets, and protocols for reproducible evaluation, and we enumerate implications and open problems.

7. External Corroboration & Implications
• E₈ as n=0 shell. Viazovska proves E₈ is optimal in 8D with density ; the 240 kissing neighbors supply canonical symmetry neighborhoods. This justifies our choice of quantizer and adjacency costs. 
• Subadditive bundling. The 2025 result that unknotting number is not additive under connected sum matches our observation that packed n=7 joins can be cheaper than the sum of parts: shared work gets reused. 
• CRT/duality and gap opening. Avila–Jitomirskaya’s solution of the Ten Martini Problem (Cantor spectrum) and recent Dry Ten Martini theorems for Sturmian Hamiltonians align with our “all gates open” regime under strict CRT labeling and duality; our legality tiling is the combinatorial twin of the spectral Cantor set. 
• Chirality no-go. Our ACOC flags chirality defects in the same cases Distler–Garibaldi proved no E₈-embedding can achieve the correct SM chirality without mirror partners: the obstruction appears as nonzero minimal receipts in both orientations. 
8. Limitations & Open Problems
• Global optimality proofs. While E₈ supports local optimality, proving global minimal work plans for long streams (with overlapping windows) is NP-hard in general; we rely on session/regime heuristics.
• Operator palette learning. Choosing when to pay the 16 + 1 vs promote to an n=5+ pack is policy-dependent; meta-learning these dials across domains is ongoing.
• Beyond E₈. For special applications (24D coding; non-Euclidean embeddings), substituting Leech or gluing constructions needs dedicated quantizers and guards.
• Physics analogies. While braiding and spectral duality analogies are persuasive, formal theorems connecting our ledger statistics to topological or spectral invariants remain to be written.
