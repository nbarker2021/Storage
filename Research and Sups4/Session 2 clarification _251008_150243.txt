3) Why exactly 8 policy channels (mathematical sketch)
We don’t assert “8” from E₈; we derive 8 from the board under constraints.
• Chamber: 4×4 torus with a fixed pal seed.
• Equivalence group : lane-preserving D₈ subgroup .
• Admissible sites are those where an insertion plus triadic mirrors (vert+horiz+one diagonal) leaves unchanged and is idempotent.
Step 1 (admissible set):
Pal constraints exclude edges; the only cells simultaneously fixable (rows & cols) by triadic mirrors are:
• Corners: ,
• Centers: . That’s 8 cells total.
Step 2 (orbit decomposition under ):
• Corners split by diagonal type:
(main diag) vs (anti diag) → 2 corner orbits.
• Centers split similarly:
(main) vs (anti) → 2 center orbits.
So 4 orbits total.
Step 3 (phase split):
Within each orbit, triadic repairs come in two mirror-phases (choose diagonal = main vs anti in the repair set), producing two inequivalent legal repairs per orbit. Hence 4 orbits × 2 phases = 8 policy channels.
This is “necessary” because:
(i) Admissibility filters the 16 cells down to exactly those 8.
(ii) Lane-preserving symmetries cannot map corners↔centers, nor main↔anti within the admissible subset.
(iii) The repair set’s diagonal choice is a real degree of freedom that doesn’t vanish under .
Therefore the 4→8 split is enforced by constraints + symmetry, not assumed.

5) MORSR specifics (algorithms)
Inputs: seed pose (pal rest), admissibility checks, , lane-preserving symmetries.
Pulse generation:
• Middle-out pulse: start at chamber center; read its local signature (lanes available, repairs needed).
• Cardinal expansion: step 1 cell north/south/east/west; perform local checks; record contracts.
• Diagonal reserve: mark diagonals but do not consummate repairs yet; note both diagonal options.
• Outside-in pulse: inject from the eight admissible cells (if known) or from the perimeter; meet the middle.
• Contract match: a lane “saturates” when inside→outside and outside→inside agree on: 
• admissibility bit,
• triadic repair choice (which diagonal),

Termination criteria:
• Completion: all 8 lanes saturated and no new admissible gates discovered in the last ring (or two full pulses produce identical contracts).
• Fail-safe cutoff: ring radius exceeds chamber diameter (2 for 4×4) without saturation → incomplete.

6) Triadic repair necessity: where we stand
• Sufficient: Triadic (vert+horiz+one diagonal) always suffices at the eight admissible cells to maintain row+col palindromy after insertion (you mirror the changed symbol across the row and column centers; the diagonal pick disambiguates lane consistency at overlaps).
• Necessity (claim to prove): On the pal seed, for those eight cells, no 1- or 2-mirror repair set preserves both row and column palindromy and idempotence.
• Status: We have empirical support on the chamber; a short case-by-case proof is within reach (enumerate the eight cells; show that fixing row pal alone/horizontal mirror will break the corresponding column pal unless the vertical mirror is also applied; then show the remaining diagonal resolves the overlap).
• What would falsify necessity: an explicit 2-mirror repair at (say) (1,1) that keeps both row+col pal and idempotence. (We haven’t found one.)
