## 4. Viscosity as a Function of Lattice Geometry

Fluid viscosity, the measure of a fluid's resistance to flow, is also shown to be an emergent property derived directly from the geometry of the E8 lattice.

-   **Lattice Spacing**: The minimum distance between E8 root vectors defines the fundamental "granularity" of the computational space.
-   **Vibration Frequency**: The average magnitude of the E8 root vectors determines the natural frequency of lattice vibrations.

Viscosity (μ) is proportional to the square of the lattice spacing multiplied by the vibration frequency. This geometric definition of viscosity allows for the derivation of a dimensionless Reynolds number analog, which correctly predicts the transition from laminar to turbulent flow.
